1 · AT&C Loss Heatmap — Which feeder loses the most energy and when?
Each cell shows the AT&C (Aggregate Technical & Commercial) loss percentage for a feeder in a given month — the share of power sent in that was never billed or collected. Red = high loss, green = low loss. Hover any cell for the exact figure.
Key findings: FDR07 (Old City West) is the worst feeder — it peaks at ~32% loss and stays red all year. FDR09 (Industrial North) is trending worse month by month due to drift. October–November is consistently the worst period across nearly every feeder. FDR05 (Harbor Road) and FDR04 (Station Yard) stay green year-round — these are the network's best performers.

2 · Feeder Deterioration — Who is getting worse vs better over 12 months?
Shows the month-on-month change in AT&C loss for each feeder across the year. Each coloured bar in the sparkline column represents one month: red bar = loss went up (worse), green bar = loss went down (better). The "12-month change" column shows the total drift from June 2025 to May 2026.
Key findings: FDR07, FDR09, and FDR13 show consistent upward drift — they have been getting worse for 5+ consecutive months with no recovery. FDR05, FDR04, and FDR03 are genuinely improving. FDR09's month-on-month rises are the steepest on the network (+4.2% in a single month).

3 · Meter Exceptions vs Commercial Loss — Does more tampering mean more revenue loss?
Each dot is one feeder. X-axis = number of meter exceptions flagged during the year (stopped meters, bypassed meters, tampered seals). Y-axis = commercial loss in ₹ Lakhs (billed but uncollected). The dashed line is the best-fit trend. Dots above the trend line have higher loss than their exceptions predict — possible under-reporting or weak enforcement.
Key findings: Correlation is very strong (r ≈ 0.91) — more meter exceptions reliably predict higher commercial loss. FDR07 and FDR13 sit in the top-right danger zone. FDR14 (Civic Hospital) is the outlier to investigate — it shows much higher loss than its exception count suggests, indicating exceptions are either going unflagged or enforcement actions are not being completed.
